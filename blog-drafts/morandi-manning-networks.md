A reading of Giorgio Morandi's work using "methods of the internet." Comparisons to recent work such as Michael Manning's Microsoft Store Paintings.

Ways of seeing and ways of working.

Work that creates dialogue within itself. Experiencing the network of images is like dropping in on a conversation that's been going on and will continue without you.

Thru networks of images the boundaries and rules are revealed through patterns. What "can't be seen" becomes seen.

Viewing Facebook profiles vs MySpace profiles vs Geocities. The internet as a place of decreasing diversity, increasing limitations on creativity through heavily curated experiences (for profit). Language and technology limitations (HTML,CSS,js) vs man made for profit limitations (Facebook, gifs disallowed, gifs censored in messenger).

Experiencing art today through google images. Googling rubens hero and Leander delivers a network of images, so that my experience goes from the many to the one. It introduces choices by the viewer "which is the correct image", size requirements, etc. Relate to this method of seeing art to engravings before photography.

Art as a set of instructions (program).
