art sightings.

Around my third year in college, I was working on a large, something over six feet painting. I was at a point in school where I had too many ideas and no conviction. This painting survived a couple of weeks, and its successor lasted a few more before I took apart the frame and tossed the canvas.

In those two weeks, a friend had visited the studio and taken this picture. That friend about six years later released an album, Now I Am Become Movement. The cover art remixed my original painting; this far in time removed from the work and that I had destoyed the painting, I no longer felt it really belonged to me.

I didn't care anything about it. I had long forgotten the original intent of the work, all I saw now was something that could vaguely be described as "cool". Months later, the album was released online through a small label. I was amused seeing my old work (and self) resurrected. I happened to google the album—as you do, googling is more familiar even when you know exactly where you need to go. Equal parts habit and flânerie.

That's when I realized something was happening: bots were listening in and reposting, rebranding, other people's work. This was nothing new to me, but in 2017 that part of the internet had been effectively sectioned off or better disguised as human. It felt very strange seeing six year old work by my college self being disseminated by bots into the opaque internet... whose primary customer certainly must be other bots. There was some kind of artistic process happening that was completely independent of me at this point. They obviously couldn't see the work or interpet it, regardless it had been swept up into their social network. I was compelled to document it, otherwise the only audience for it would be the bots.
